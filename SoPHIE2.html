Three Practical Examples



Here are three examples demonstrating how the game works.

In each round of the game, you will see a table displaying different investment level options along with their corresponding percentage of savings. It's important to note that the savings range for each investment level will vary from round to round due to market fluctuations. Some markets offer more stable savings, where the potential savings range is narrower, while others can be more volatile, with a much wider range of possible savings.Additionally, the percentage of saving which depends on investment efficiency, may also change. Your task is to decide how much to invest, with options ranging from $0 to $4,000.

Now, let's analyze the process of the game for three different senarios, one in a more stable market, another in a more volatile market, and the last one in the market with different level of efficiency(percentage saving), based on three sample tables.


Example 1:

Imagine we encounter the following table in the game, representing a more stable(narrower range of possible savings) market.

Suppose we choose an investment level of $3,200.

  • According to this table, this investment corresponds to a 16.97% percentage of savings on electricity costs.
  • The potential savings will fall within a specific range ($3,224, $3,564), with the most likely savings being $3,394 .
  • In the best-case scenario, our Net Saving on this investment will be $364 , while in the worst-case scenario, it will be $24 .

    • Now the game determines(randomly) the realized savings for our investment level and informs us that it is $3,300.

    Based on this, we can now calculate the Net Saving on our investment.

    Net Saving = Realized Saving-EE Investment


     $3,300 - $3,200 = $100
